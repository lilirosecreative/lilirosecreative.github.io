THE DESIGNER

My name is Liliana Moreno and I am a third year graphic design student studying at Murdoch University. I am a very creative person, and since a young age, have always had a passion for all things art and design.

My works, created through in-depth research and development, always take people into consideration and aim to address problems in exciting and innovative ways.

Improving waste management at Murdoch University

The 2020 sustainability representative from the guild had come across the issue of contamination in the recycling bins and realised the current signage in place was ineffective. In helping to resolve this problem, my objective for this project was to redesign the current bin signage for both recycling and landfill.

Making information easy to find with the new Fremantle Arts Centre microsite

For a university project, I created a microsite for the The Fremantle arts centre. The popularity of the FAC’s concert series has grown immensely in the past few years and with users finding it hard to access event details. I created a microsite to focus on the unique concert events and other experiences the venue has to offer.

Life Fit - Addressing an uneven work-life balance

There is no doubt that being a University Student is difficult, having to make time for study, work, socialising, exercise and more. Sometimes, time management can be hard and finding the right work-life balance between the different aspects of your life even more so. I created the concept and visual identity for app ‘Life Fit’ for the purpose of helping university students to keep a healthy balance between the different aspects of their lives.
